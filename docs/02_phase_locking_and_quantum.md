## 2. Classical Entrainment vs. Quantum Entanglement

In classical mechanics, mechanical oscillators (e.g., pendulum clocks or acoustic strings) exchange energy through a physical coupling medium until their frequencies synchronize.

In the **Dynamic Phase** architecture, quantum entanglement is the microscopic equivalent of this entrainment principle, occurring within the fundamental subatomic wave substrate.

### 2.1 The Phase-Locked Loop Equation

Two coupled systems $A$ and $B$ achieve state locking when the phase difference ($\Delta \phi$) stabilizes over time ($\tau$):

$$\frac{d(\Delta \phi)}{d\tau} = \omega_A - \omega_B - K \sin(\Delta \phi)$$

*Regular text version:*
d(Delta phi) / d(Tau) = omega_A - omega_B - K * sin(Delta phi)

Where:
* $\omega_A, \omega_B$ (omega_A, omega_B): Uncoupled intrinsic angular frequencies of nodes $A$ and $B$.
* $K$: Coupling strength coefficient of the underlying field.
* $\tau$ (Tau): Emergent time delay parameter.

When coupling strength $K > \vert{}\omega_A - \omega_B\vert{}$, the system locks into phase alignment, eliminating state variance ($\Delta \phi \to 0$).
